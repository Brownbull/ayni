## Executive Summary

This user research report provides a deep understanding of Chilean retail SMB decision-makers who would purchase and use a business analytics platform. Based on market research, behavioral patterns, and Jobs-to-be-Done analysis, we've identified three primary user personas and their key motivations, pain points, and decision criteria.

### Key User Insights

**Primary User Profile:**
- **Role**: Business Owner or Operations Manager
- **Company**: 3-10 location retail business, 10-50 employees
- **Age**: 35-55 years old
- **Tech Comfort**: Moderate (uses smartphones, basic SaaS tools)
- **Pain**: "I have data everywhere but no insights"
- **Goal**: "Make confident, data-driven expansion decisions"

**Critical Findings:**

1. **Jobs-to-be-Done**: Users hire analytics to gain **control** and **confidence**, not just data
2. **Purchase Triggers**: Expansion decisions (new location), performance problems (underperforming stores), competitive pressure
3. **Adoption Barriers**: Complexity fear, budget constraints, time scarcity
4. **Success Metrics**: Time saved (10+ hours/month), better inventory decisions, location performance visibility
5. **Decision Process**: Owner decides alone or with operations manager; 1-2 week evaluation; monthly budget <$100

## 3. Behavioral Insights

### Technology Adoption Patterns

**SMB Retail SaaS Adoption Behavior:**
- **Discovery**: Google search > Word-of-mouth > LinkedIn ads > Trade shows
- **Trial**: 83% start with free trial before buying [Source: Market Research]
- **Purchase Cycle**: 1-2 weeks (NOT enterprise 6-month cycle)
- **Buying Committee**: Owner + Operations Manager (2 people max)
- **Budget**: Monthly expense, not CapEx (no CFO approval needed)

**Chilean-Specific Behaviors:**
- **Payment Preference**: Credit card (44% of e-commerce) or digital wallets
- **Language**: Spanish required, English okay as secondary
- **Support Hours**: Chilean business hours (9am-6pm CLT)
- **Social Proof**: Local testimonials valued > international case studies
- **Relationship-Driven**: Personal relationships matter, cold sales harder

### Price Sensitivity

**Willingness to Pay Dynamics:**
- **$0-30/month**: "Easy yes" - low consideration
- **$30-75/month**: "Worth it if it saves time" - moderate consideration
- **$75-150/month**: "Needs clear ROI" - high consideration, may consult accountant
- **$150+/month**: "Too expensive" - most SMBs reject

**Reference Prices:**
- Accounting software: $30-70/month (they're paying this)
- POS system: $50-200/month (they're paying this)
- Our product: Should feel similar to these ($50-100 range)

**Budget Psychology:**
- SMBs think monthly, not annually (unlike enterprise)
- $79/month feels like $80 (round number)
- Annual prepay needs 20%+ discount to motivate
- Hidden fees are deal-breakers (trust issue)

### Decision-Making Process

**Typical Purchase Flow:**

**Week 1:**
- Monday: Realizes need ("I need to figure out why Store 3 is underperforming")
- Tuesday-Thursday: Googles, asks peers, finds 2-3 options
- Friday: Signs up for free trials

**Week 2:**
- Monday: Tests our tool + competitor(s)
- Tuesday: Has operations manager test
- Wednesday: Compares pricing, features
- Thursday: Makes decision, upgrades
- Friday: Onboards team

**Influencers:**
- Operations Manager: HIGH influence (daily user)
- Accountant/Bookkeeper: MEDIUM influence (may recommend tools)
- Peers: HIGH influence (word-of-mouth trusted)
- POS Provider: MEDIUM influence (if partnership exists)

## 4. Pain Point Hierarchy

### Critical Pains (High Urgency + High Impact)

**1. Multi-Location Performance Invisibility**
- **Quote**: "I can't quickly see which stores are doing well and which aren't"
- **Impact**: Delays problem detection by weeks, costs $5-20K in lost revenue
- **Frequency**: Daily/weekly concern
- **Willingness to Pay**: $100+/month to solve
- **Current Workaround**: Manual Excel compilation (3-4 hours/week)

**2. Expansion Decision Uncertainty**
- **Quote**: "I don't know which location to replicate for my next store"
- **Impact**: $50-100K investment decision, make or break business
- **Frequency**: 1-2x per year, but high-stakes
- **Willingness to Pay**: $500-1000 one-time for this insight
- **Current Workaround**: Gut feel, asking peers, demographic research

**3. Competitive Blindness**
- **Quote**: "I have no idea if my performance is good compared to similar retailers"
- **Impact**: May be underperforming vs. market but not know
- **Frequency**: Constant background anxiety
- **Willingness to Pay**: $50+/month for benchmarking
- **Current Workaround**: None (impossible to get competitor data)

### Important Pains (Medium Urgency + High Impact)

**4. Inventory Optimization Guesswork**
- **Impact**: $20-50K annual waste in overstock + stockouts
- **Frequency**: Weekly inventory decisions
- **Willingness to Pay**: $50-100/month
- **Current Workaround**: Gut feel, historical averages

**5. Product Profitability Uncertainty**
- **Impact**: May be promoting low-margin products
- **Frequency**: Monthly product decisions
- **Willingness to Pay**: $30-50/month
- **Current Workaround**: Revenue-based decisions (ignoring profit)

### Annoying Pains (Low Urgency + Medium Impact)

**6. Manual Reporting Time Waste**
- **Impact**: 5-10 hours/week operations manager time
- **Frequency**: Weekly/monthly
- **Willingness to Pay**: $30-50/month
- **Current Workaround**: Accept as cost of business

## 5. Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis
